Project

  • Simulation Studies on Electromagnetic Forming of Dual Phase Steel Friction Stir Welded Blanks

    Project Focus: Investigation of Friction Stir Welding (FSW) and Electromagnetic Forming (EMF) processes. Utilization of ANSYS software for simulation, emphasizing the synergy of FSW and EMF in sheet metal joining...

    Objectives:

    • Optimization of key FSW parameters (Tool Rotational Speed, Welding Speed, Tool Tilt Angle).
    • Optimization of key EMF parameters (Input Voltage, Current, Coil Design).
    • Unlocking the full potential of FSW and EMF for creating strong welded joints and enhancing structural integrity.

    Anticipated Outcomes:

    • Creation of robust welded joints through parameter optimization.
    • Identification and mitigation strategies for potential failure modes.
    • Enhanced reliability of both EMF and FSW processes.

    Broader Significance:

    • Versatile applicability of EMF and FSW across aerospace, automotive, and manufacturing industries.
    • Utilization of ANSYS software for simulation promises innovative solutions to sheet metal joining challenges.

    Keywords:

    Electromagnetic Forming, Friction Stir Welding, Simulation Studies, Dual Phase Steels, Parameter Optimization, Sheet Metal Joining, Industrial Applications.

    Conclusion:

    This ongoing research project explores the dynamic interplay between Friction Stir Welding and Electromagnetic Forming processes, employing advanced simulation techniques. The focus on parameter optimization aims to unlock the full potential of these processes, promising robust welded joints with enhanced structural integrity. The anticipated outcomes, including the identification and mitigation of failure modes, hold the potential to significantly advance sheet metal joining techniques across diverse industrial applications.
